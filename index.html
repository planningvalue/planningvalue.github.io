Tell Me More.

I feel like it is frustrating and difficult for a lot of us to design and achieve a financial goal on our own. For one thing, personal finance is very personal! It’s difficult to view your situation/expenses/and goals objectively or to even carve out the time to sit down and think about them at all!

I graduated from MIT with a degree in Brain and Cognitive Science and a concentration in Economics. My educational background got me thinking a lot about why people behave the way they do and how we can get a kickstart to tap into our internal motivation to reach our own goals. I think it is critical to point out that no financial coach can pick your goals for you, they have to be 100% your own or there’s no chance we’re going to be able to help you succeed.

I really believe there’s a better way to do this - specifically treating financial goals and action plans like multimillion dollar tech projects and assigning a project manager (aka me, your financial coach!) to help clarify:

  1. 1. What the goal is.
  2. 2. What the definition of success is.
  3. 3. What milestones we’ll hit.
  4. 4. How we will track progress.
  5. 5. How to influence clients to get motivated to put in the work (oh yes there will be work!) to make it happen.


    6. I’ve been following this process for years with software engineers, execs, designers and marketers to produce tangible results. It is my life’s work to work with people 1:1 to do the same thing for their financial lives.
